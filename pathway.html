Press Release · April 2026
A New Solution for Heat Stress Resilience in Bangladesh's Livestock Industry

In Bangladesh's monsoon climate, livestock farming faces a relentless challenge: heat stress. With the Temperature-Humidity Index (THI) consistently exceeding the critical threshold of 72 across all regions — and climbing to 77–81 from May through September — animals are pushed into moderate-to-severe thermal stress, with feed intake dropping by as much as 30%.

To address this, RS Poultry has partnered with Pathway Intermediates to officially launch STREX in the Bangladeshi market. STREX is an all-in-one solution built for hot, humid climates — using fermented functional sugar to deliver a "Cooling Sensation" that reduces heat sensitivity via receptor modulation, normalizes feed intake, and protects productivity. It also provides antioxidant and antipyretic effects while supporting gut health and immunity, even at high THI levels.

"STREX has been tested under our research farm. In dairy cattle trials, we've seen an 8.1% increase in milk yield and improved udder health even during the hottest summer months. For poultry, it has improved feed intake by 4.1% and survival rates by 3.1% in environments with THI levels as high as 85. We're incredibly excited to bring these results to the Bangladeshi market." — Ness, Product Manager, Pathway Intermediates
